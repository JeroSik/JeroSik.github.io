Purdue USB Help Room

Jan 2019 - Present

Purdue USB is the Undergraduate Student Board for the Department of Computer Science (CS) at Purdue University. Part of the goal for Purdue USB is to help CS students in various ways. One of the ways is to host a free help room for introductory CS classes. The Purdue USB help room answers questions to students in CS 17700 (Programming with Python), CS 18000 (Problem Solving and Object-Oriented Programming), CS 18200 (Discrete Mathematics), and CS 24000 (Programming in C).

Undergraduate Teaching Assistant CS 18200 (Discrete Mathematics)

Jan 2019 - Present

As an undergraduate teaching assistant (UTA), I help the department faculty with preparing and reviewing practice problems by crafting and covering course content. Additionally, I facilitate discussion and solve problems to around 20 students in multiple 'Practice. Study. Observation.' sessions. Course topics for discrete mathematics include basic logic and mathematics language, proof techniques, algorithms, basic number theory, basic counting, discrete probability, trees, boolean algebra, combinatorial circuits, finite state machines, and context-free grammars and languages.
